MelKoop

Real-time speech enhancement for legacy low-bitrate vocoder chains and low-power edge devices requires models that are ultra-compact, strictly causal, and stable under long-horizon streaming. Existing lightweight systems are usually optimized on short utterances, while their long-stream behavior and compatibility with downstream speech codecs are often left implicit. In this paper, we propose MelKoop, a causal complex-STFT speech enhancement architecture that combines compact subband processing with an input-driven Koopman temporal core. The core uses two-dimensional Koopman rotation--decay state pairs with explicit input injection, giving a low-cost recurrent memory in which the state carried from previous frames is forced to decay rather than grow. A short-memory FIR path responds to local transients, while a bounded Mel-envelope branch applies a small correction to slow spectral-envelope trajectories without replacing the STFT-domain enhancement path. The model is trained with a reconstruction-dominant objective, supplemented by low-weight perceptual, intelligibility, and stability regularizers. MelKoop is evaluated on VoiceBank+DEMAND, VB-DemandEx, DNS Challenge data over long-stream drift diagnostics and a representative legacy low-bitrate vocoder chain. The results show that MelKoop operates in the very low-parameter and low-MAC regime while retaining competitive objective quality and stable behavior during continuous streaming.

Model summary

MelKoop is a compact causal speech-enhancement front end operating in the complex STFT domain. It combines a lightweight subband encoder, GraphFreq message passing over compressed frequency bands, a hybrid FIR--Koopman temporal core, a small Mel-envelope correction branch, and a complex-mask decoder.
